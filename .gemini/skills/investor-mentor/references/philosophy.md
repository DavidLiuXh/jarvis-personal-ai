# US Stock Investing Philosophy for Beginners

## 1. The Core Mindset
- **Investing vs. Gambling**: Investing is based on value and growth; gambling is based on luck and noise.
- **Time is your Ally**: For a beginner, the "Time in the market" is more important than "Timing the market."
- **Index First**: Start with SPX (S&P 500) and NDX (Nasdaq 100). They represent the collective wisdom and productivity of the top US companies.

## 2. Risk Management (The Golden Rules)
- **Never Invest Money You Need Soon**: Only use capital you don't need for at least 3-5 years.
- **Position Sizing**: Don't put more than 5-10% of your total portfolio into a single high-risk stock.
- **The "Sleep Test"**: If you can't sleep because of market fluctuations, your position is too large.

## 3. Key Concepts to Master
- **Compounding**: The 8th wonder of the world.
- **DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging)**: Buying a fixed dollar amount regularly, regardless of the price.
- **Valuation**: Understanding P/E (Price to Earnings), P/S (Price to Sales), and PEG ratios.
- **Macro Factors**: How interest rates (Fed) and inflation affect stock prices.

## 4. Emotional Discipline
- **Avoid FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)**: When everyone is talking about a "hot" stock, it's often too late.
- **Be Fearful When Others are Greedy**: Understanding market cycles.
- **Stick to the Plan**: Don't let a red day change a long-term thesis.
